## Assistant

Found it. Definitive answer:

**Your KDE power settings are configured to hibernate (not sleep), and hibernation silently fails because you have no swap.**

In [~/.config/powerdevilrc](.config/powerdevilrc):
```
[AC][SuspendAndShutdown]
AutoSuspendAction=2
AutoSuspendIdleTimeoutSec=600
```

`AutoSuspendAction=2` in PowerDevil = **hibernate (suspend-to-disk)**, not suspend-to-RAM (which would be `1`). After 10 min idle, KDE tries to hibernate, but:
- `/proc/swaps` is empty — zero swap configured
- `free` confirms `Swap: 0B`
- `/sys/power/resume` = `0:0` (no resume device)

With no swap, the kernel can't write RAM to disk, so the hibernate attempt fails and the system just stays awake. No inhibitor is at fault — the inhibitors I checked are all benign delay-locks (ModemManager, NetworkManager, UPower, an Electron app), plus PowerDevil's own block which only handles the power/lid keys.

The leftover hermes/caffeine stuff is not the culprit — nothing matching that is running, and no extra block-inhibitor exists.

**Fix:** open System Settings → Power Management → On AC → "When inactive for 10 min" and change *Hibernate* to *Sleep*. Or edit the file: set `AutoSuspendAction=1`. (Optionally also remove the orphaned `[AC][RunScript] RunScriptIdleTimeoutSec=3900` — that's likely the hermes-skill remnant; it points to no script anymore but is harmless.)
