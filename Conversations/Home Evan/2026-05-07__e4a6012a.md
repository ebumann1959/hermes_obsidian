## The Three Layers Worth Building

### 1. Knowledge Base (Obsidian + static docs)
This is the "battle book brain." Markdown chunks well for RAG — low friction to get this live first. This is your doctrine layer: *what arguments exist and how to make them.*

### 2. Precedent Layer (ClaimWizard + Gmail)
This is the "we won this before" layer. Match current job characteristics (carrier, line items, denial language) against past successful supplements. Huge leverage — most rebuttals are variations of something she's already won.

### 3. Financial Grounding (QuickBooks)
Supplement pricing arguments need to cite what the market actually pays. This layer prevents Claude from hallucinating numbers.

## Key Workflows to Automate

2. **Line item argument** — given a specific denied line item, pull all known justification for it (Xactimate basis, code, precedent)
3. **Supplement scope builder** — given job photos + initial scope, identify likely missed line items
4. **Carrier-specific playbooks** — carriers have patterns; RAG over her history can surface what works against State Farm vs. Allstate vs. whoever
