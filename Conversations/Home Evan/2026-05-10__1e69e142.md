## Assistant

Based on the skill, here's what actually runs under the hood:

1. `keep-awake start` is called, which runs:
   ```
   kde-inhibit --power -- bash -c 'while true; do sleep 30; done'
   ```
2. `kde-inhibit --power` talks directly to KDE's PowerDevil over D-Bus and registers a power inhibitor — telling PowerDevil "don't suspend while this process is alive."
3. The `while true; do sleep 30; done` loop just keeps the process alive indefinitely so the inhibitor stays registered.
4. When you run `keep-awake stop`, it kills that process, the inhibitor is automatically released, and PowerDevil resumes normal sleep behavior.

So it's purely session-scoped and process-scoped — no config files touched, no reboots affected. Machine sleeps normally the moment the process dies.
