## Assistant

Yes, I2C runs over two GPIO pins on the Pi's 40-pin header:

- **GPIO 2 (pin 3)** — SDA (data)
- **GPIO 3 (pin 5)** — SCL (clock)

Those are the hardware I2C bus (I2C-1). Always available, no configuration needed beyond enabling I2C in `raspi-config`.

**The INA219 wiring:**

The sensor goes **inline on the power input** — between your USB-C/micro-USB power supply and the Pi's 5V rail. You're measuring the current the whole board draws from the supply.

```
Power supply → INA219 VIN+ / VIN- → Pi 5V rail
INA219 SDA  → GPIO 2 (pin 3)
INA219 SCL  → GPIO 3 (pin 5)
INA219 GND  → any GND pin
INA219 VCC  → 3.3V (pin 1)
```

The INA219 has a small shunt resistor (usually 0.1Ω on the breakout) that the current passes through. It measures the voltage drop across that shunt and reports current + bus voltage over I2C. The Pi then reads watts = volts × amps directly.

If you tap the 5V GPIO pins instead you only measure what flows through those pins, which misses some of the draw.

The cleanest physical setup is a small INA219 breakout wired with short leads to a micro-USB pigtail inline on the power cable. Takes about 20 minutes with a soldering iron.
