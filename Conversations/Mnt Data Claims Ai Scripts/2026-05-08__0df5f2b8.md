## Core O&P Arguments

**1. General Contractor Coordination Requirement**
The claim requires coordination of multiple trades (roofing, drywall, paint, gutters, etc.). A GC is needed to schedule, supervise, and warranty the work — this is precisely what O&P compensates for.

**2. Sub-Contractor Bid Documentation**
Per *Win The Game > Documentation*: Provide actual sub-contractor bids for each trade. This shifts the burden of proof to the adjuster and demonstrates real coordination costs — not theoretical ones.

**3. O&P Applies to the Entire Scope**
Per *Ltr to ump re biased award.docx*: O&P must be calculated on the **total of all repairs**, including roofing. Excluding any trade from the O&P calculation is not supported by any objective third-party source and misrepresents the insured's loss.

**4. Industry & Legal Precedent**
Per *CatAdjuster.org Forum*: Courts have ruled (Muskegon, MA class action) that withholding O&P until a signed GC contract is in hand puts the homeowner at a negotiating disadvantage — O&P must be paid upfront when a GC is reasonably anticipated.

**5. It Is Not Optional for Qualifying Claims**
Per *CatAdjuster.org Forum*: "It is NOT ok to exclude O&P if it qualifies." Carrier client instructions to exclude it do not override proper claims handling procedure under state compliance rules.

**6. Complexity / Multi-Trade Trigger**
When a claim involves 3 or more trades, industry standard is that a GC is required. Document every trade involved and make this explicit in your estimate narrative.
