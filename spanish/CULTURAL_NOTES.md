# Cultural Notes — Mexican Spanish

## Why This Matters
Textbook Spanish and Mexican street Spanish are very different. Native speakers will use formality levels, slang, and expressions that courses don't teach. Understanding *why* things are said a certain way helps them stick.

## Key Themes for Your Learning

### Informal Register Dominates
Mexican friends will almost never use formal "usted" with you unless being deliberately polite. "Tú" and informal verbs everywhere.

### Common Mexican Slang You'll Hear
- Güero/güera — blonde/white-skinned person (not an insult)
- Weón/güeón — dude, guy (very common, like "dude" or "guy")
- Estar pedo — to be drunk
- Estar crudo — to be hungover  
- Lágrima — a shot of tequila
- Mecaso / no mames — expressions of surprise/disbelief
- ¡Órale! — agreement, surprise, "let's go!"
- Está gacho — it's bad/lame
- No hay pedo — no problem / it's cool
- Echele ganas — give it effort, try hard

### Tense Mixing
Mexicans often use present perfect where English would use simple past ("Ya me explicó" instead of "Me explicó"). This is normal and common.

## Cultural Context for Your Friend
- Self-employed electrician
- Father of a young daughter

- Mexican, likely from a working-class background
